About Me

Personal Profile

Sidewalk Labs | NYPD | DANY | JJAY M.A. | Yale B.A. | data scientist | technology enthusiast | New Yorker

Born and raised in New York City, I've always been interested in cities and how they work. I've spent the last few years working in law enforcement in city government (in NYC) where I've supported criminal investigations, conducted large data analytics projects and helped devise crime strategies. I've found that I'm most passionate about exploring opportunities at the intersection of public safety and technology. Currently, I serve as the Lead for Public Safety & Criminal Justice at Sidewalk Labs, an Alphabet company that works with cities to address big urban problems.

I graduated from Yale University in 2013 where I majored in Political Science. During my senior year, I developed an interest in law enforcement after taking a class called "Policing America." After graduation, I was hired by the Manhattan DA's Office as an Intelligence Analyst in the Crime Strategies Unit ("CSU"). The unit primarily collects, organizes, and analyzes intelligence to enhance prosecutions. While in CSU, I worked on a variety of cases from gang conspiracies to narcotics investigations to homicides. The innovative work of CSU has led district attorney's offices across the country to embrace the intelligence-driven prosecution model championed by the Manhattan District Attorney Cyrus Vance, Jr.

While working at the Manhattan DA's Office, I enrolled in the Criminal Justice graduate program at John Jay College of Criminal Justice. I earned my Master's Degree in June 2015 with a specialization in Investigative Techniques. At around the same time, I became interested in data science and programming, which I regrettably did not study in college. I refined my analytical skills while taking General Assembly's Data Science course, which I later helped teach as the "expert-in-residence." I am proficient in Python and SQL and dabble in several other languages (HTML, CSS, Ruby, VBA).

Immediately prior to joining Sidewalk Labs, I was employed by the New York Police Department. I first worked in the Office of the Deputy Commissioner Strategic Initiatives (DCSI) where I focused on developing strategies to enhance crime intelligence efforts. I provided investigative analytical support to various units, particularly those focused on violent crime and gang-related investigations. After a brief stint working for an intelligence unit in the Bronx, I came back to 1 Police Plaza to work in the Office of the Deputy Commissioner Operations, the NYPD's crime strategy and "CompStat" unit. My work largely consisted of using data-driven methods to develop new, innovative crime reduction strategies. I was responsible for a variety of large scale analytical and technology projects and supported weekly CompStat meetings.
